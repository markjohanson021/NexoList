11 Most Anticipated Video Games of 2019

published on Sunday, 18 August 2019

What a time to be alive! Video gaming is at an all time high and the video gaming industry has even surpassed the value of the music and video industry combined. We’ve seen some great video games in the past, and it looks like 2019 is going to raise the bar even higher. Let’s take a look at some projects that may or may not arrive in the next year.

Table of Contents

  • Red Dead Redemption 2 – PC Version
  • Code Vein
  • Rage 2
  • Kingdom Hearts 3
  • Ori And The Will of the Wisps
  • The Wolf Among Us – Season 2
  • Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice
  • Devil May Cry 5
  • Cyberpunk 2077
  • Monster Hunter World: Iceborne

The Last of Us 2

The first one was critically acclaimed success and ever since the trailer for the second one dropped, people have been anxious for it. If it’s even half as good as the first one, it’ll blow everyone’s mind.

Red Dead Redemption 2 – PC Version

It’s Rockstar – you know this is coming sooner or later. PC players deserve to be cowboys too.

Code Vein

An anime/vampire Dark Souls? This looks like an action-packed heap of fun, with – of course – infuriating boss fights.

Rage 2

The first one wasn’t all that known, but it was a more than decent game. The second one looks like it’s going to turn it up a notch, so be sure to keep an eye on this one. It’s like a more realistic version of Borderlands!

Kingdom Hearts 3

Disney meets Final Fantasy – and we’ve been waiting for the third one for quite a while! Well, technically it’s more like the fifth (or sixth?) one, but you know what I mean.

Ori And The Will of the Wisps

This gorgeous platformer is getting a sequel that’ll be sure to keep us entertained for several hours once more.

The Wolf Among Us – Season 2

No one expected this one to come, especially since the first was released back in 2013. If you’re into storytelling and a darker version of classic fairy tales, this one might be for you.

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice

It’s the new game by FromSoft, the guys that gave us Dark Souls. Not much is known about it, apart from the fact that it’s sure to cause some controllers being thrown against walls again.

Devil May Cry 5

And we all thought this series was getting rebooted after DmC, huh? Well, apparently they’re moving on with number five now, and Nero and Dante look ready for action!

Cyberpunk 2077

An open world cyberpunk first-person RPG from the developers that made The Witcher 3. This can only be good. Very good.

Monster Hunter World: Iceborne

The base game was Capcom’s best selling game to date and the expansion is going to add more of the stuff we love: monsters to carve up for weapons and armor!
